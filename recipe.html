Abacha Chukwu

A Yummy Abacha, carefully made from dried shredded cassava and properly mixed source. One of the Igbos favourite dish.

Section one: Recipe information

Preparation time: 30 minutes

Cooking time: 30 minutes

Number of servings: One person

Difficulty level: Intermediate

Section two:Ingredients Section

  • Dried shredded cassava (abacha)Two full hands
  • Palm oil(half cup)
  • Edible potash(one)
  • Garden eggs,utazi leaves,Onions
  • Ugba
  • Calabsh nutmeg
  • Ogiri(one small spoon)
  • Fresh fish
  • Crayfish
  • Pepper(one Tbsp)
  • Salt (one Tbsp)
  • Seasoning cubes (One cube)

Section Four: Instructions

  1. Dissolve Potash, sieve the water out and keep aside.
  2. Smoke the Fish.
  3. Roast and grind the Calabash nutmeg.
  4. Put the dried Abacha in a hot water and remove after 2 minutes.
  5. In a pot,add the dissolve potash.
  6. Put the oil and mix together.
  7. Add maggi, salt and pepper, including the grinded nutmeg and ogiri. mix properly.
  8. Add onion to the pot and mix properly.
  9. add Abacha to the mixed sauce and mix properly.
  10. Test for salt and garnish with Utazi leave and sliced garden egg.
  11. Serve and enjoy with your smoked Fish.

TIPS

For a better enjoyment, compliment it with a fresh palm wine , direct from source.

Nutritional value

It's a good source of carbohydrates, providing quick energy, and can also offer fiber, protein, healthy fats, and essential vitamins and minerals depending on the ingredients used in preparation.
